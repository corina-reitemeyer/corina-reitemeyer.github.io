Sprint 3 - Emotional Intelligence

An exploration on why emotional intelligence is important as a Software Developer, and how it differs from Intellectual quotient (IQ).

1 April 2024

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence (or short: EQ) is the skill of being able to recognise, understand, regulate and express your emotions, whilst also using social awareness in problem-solving. Furthermore, it helps us to use our emotions to facilitate healthy, positive social communication and to consider how others might feel and how we relate to them.

How is EQ different to IQ?

Intelligence quotient, or IQ, is defined as a measured number (usually acquired through a standardised test e.g. Mensa) and is used to determine a person's overall mental ability - usually referring to visual-spatial processing, knowledge of the world, working memory and quantitative reasoning. It is often seen as an ability of measuring someones cognitive abilities and their problem-solving skills. It is said that IQ is perceived to affect academics, expertise, critical thinking, and logic. IQ is still seen as an important element of success, especially in an academic context.

Whilst there are arguments that people inherit their IQ score to a certain degree, which gets influenced through environmental factors, EQ on the other hand is a learned ability which we learn from others and emulate in our environment (as early as childhood). Even if we end up with a low EQ to begin with due to a lack of role-models, it is a set of skills that can not only be newly obtained but can be improved on without needing a solid starting point.

Why is EQ important?

Usually everyone will have different strengths, whether its leaning more towards IQ compared to EQ or vice versa. The same can be said for the traits and skills of each category. Emotional intelligence is important due to the vast influence it has on our social interactions with people and the relationship to ourselves - especially when it comes to mental health and decision-making. It allows us to build stronger relationships and create self-awareness. It is said that if IQ and EQ exist in tandem, that they are most effective when build off one another. A successful person cannot exist without one or the other.
