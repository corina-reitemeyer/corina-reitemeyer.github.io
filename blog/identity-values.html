Sprint 1 - Identity, Values & Strengths

My musings around my identity, my strengths and values and how they will influence my learning at Dev Acdademy.

20 March 2024

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

I had a situation a year ago, where two parties had a serious conflict, and I knew of person more closely than the other. I was in a somewhat authoritative position at the time. I decided to hand-over the responsibility to someone in the same position as mine, who was able to stay neutral in the matter as I realised that I would be too biased too keep the conflict-resolution process fair for everyone. I value fairness and honesty very highly, so making that decision wasn’t difficult if it was the best for everyone involved.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

I grew up as NZ Pakeha in Germany, with German and Kiwi culture, which in some scenarios are complete opposites. So I noticed when living in Germany, my values and identity where more closely aligned with a German way of being, compared to me now living in New Zealand for over 10 years and some of those customs having shifted as a result. For example, when I moved to New Zealand, I valued punctuality so much that I was 15 minutes early to everything, including birthdays! Meanwhile lots of my Kiwi friends and family members ended up being 15 - 30mins late which would annoy the heck out of me. 12 years later I’m still relatively on time, but I’ve become a lot more relaxed in that sense - up to 15 minutes is ok and I might be 10 minutes late as well if the occasion is informal. This is probably due to me realising I cannot change the people around me, so I adapted.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

My strengths are Learning, Focus, Discipline, Input and being a Relator (via CliftonStrength).

My main values are Fairness, Independence and Honesty.

I can imagine that my strengths will help me keep myself accountable when learning at Dev Academy, and doing so independantly as well as in a group by giving and receiving support, and generally sharing my ideas and finding with others.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

I recently learned that I have dyscalculia, which is a form of dyslexia that affects me with processing numbers, understanding math-based concepts, estimating distance and time. It’s still very under-researched and therefore I am still trying to understand how it affects me. I do know that I get anxiety when having to face strings of numbers and calculations, which is one of the reasons why I ended up studying design over a computer science degree. However I also know that I have the power to accept and acknowledge this form of neurodivergence and find ways around it when learning new code language than let it restrict me. I probably would embrace less number-heavy code language and perhaps focus on client-based coding. We will see.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

I teach Cuban Salsa once a week. As a junior teacher I sometimes encountered different types of tension or resistance - sometimes it comes down to a lack of respect/trust of them knowing that I don’t have heaps of experience than other teachers at the school, resulting in a hesitancy of taking my advice or feedback (understandable in a way, but none the less quite unfortunate). Other times it’s the result of their own ego getting in the way or the fear of expression and therefore resisting collaboration or feedback. Other times it’s because I over-estimated their abilities and the student didn’t quite know how to incorporate feedback, so they freeze or shut down.

One strategy I try is to rephrase how I communicate. Whether it’s to simplify the instructions or use more active language to appear more direct. Sometimes I have to talk more passively, as being direct can backfire occasionally on some personality types. Sometimes I use metaphors to allow students to think about a dance move in a different way, which can sometimes help prevent the “overthinking” trap. To sum it up, if the first means of communication failed, I try a different way and see if it improved the resistance. More often than not, being empathetic and trying to relate to the other person helps them understand that at the end of the day we are on the same team and I’m not trying to fight them.

What I would do now is also allowing people to have space to re-center and give them a bit of time. If it’s due to trust, or because they feel a bit overwhelmed, time is sometimes the key solution especially if they had a difficult, full-on day or they just got to know you.
